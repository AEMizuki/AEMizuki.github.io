Understanding What's Behind The Creation Of Vocaloid Music

Culture Of Vocaloid

How did Vocaloid Music come about?

The first vocaloid software was created in 2004 named "VOCALOID1", developed by Yamaha Corporation

The synthesizer(VOCALOID1) was used to let users input melodies and lyrics to create songs using virtual voices

There were 3 main voicebanks introduced at this time:

  1. Leon and Lola (English)
  2. Miriam (based on a singer)

At this time, vocaloid was still rather unpopular due to robotic vocals and no engaging design of characters

A sudden burst in popularity for vocaloid came about in 2007 with the introduction of Hatsune Miku using a new engine named "VOCALOID2"

Miku was designed in an anime-style with a story and an identity which gave users not just a software but an idol that they could build stories and songs around

A Japanese platform called Nico Nico Douga allowed creators to upload and share their original song productions

Over time, viral hits began to pop up, such as:

  • Melt (by ryo)
  • World Is Mine(by ryo)
  • Ievan Polkka

The fan culture exploded and people began to make art, music videos, remixes, fanfics and games related to Vocaloid

By this time, the vocaloid characters have expanded beyond solely Hatsune Miku to characters such as Kagamine Rin, GUMI and Megurine Luka until eventually Vocaloid has become what it is today

Live Performances With Vocaloid

With the advancement of technology these days, vocaloid performances have become a common event for fans to experience. These concerts involve 3-dimensional projections of the vocaloid characters, most commonly, Hatsune Miku

These performances are also usually accompanied by human bands while the idol, in this case Hatsune Miku, moves, sings, dances and interacts with the audience

These vocaloid characters are animated in 3D using MikuMikuDance (MMD) or a custom software where they are projected onto transparent screens during the live performances to create the illusion for the audience that the character is on stage

You may wonder, how do the characters sing if they are only a projection? Well, it is quite simple actually. The Vocaloid's vocals which are pre-programmed are just played in sync with the visuals while a real band or orchestra play the instrumental part of the song, creating the idea that the projection of the Vocaloid is actually singing

Some examples of vocaloid live performances that have taken place are:

  1. Magical Mirai
  2. Miku Expo
  3. Project DIVA Live

Vocaloid Synthesizer Technology

How Does It Work?

To start things off, users install the synthesizer software of their choice (the different kinds of synthesizer software can be found below)

Given a variety of voicebanks in the software, the standard flow of using these synthesizers to make songs is about typing out lyrics and creating a melody which the vocaloid (depending on the used voicebank) will sing to

The voicebanks are created based on human recordings, where a voice actor sings basic sounds (phonemes) of different pitches, tones and articulations for a larger variety which are then processed into a voicebank

What are the different types of Vocaloid Synthesizers?

Some of the more commonly known synthesizers are:

  • VOCALOID
  • UTAU
  • CeVIO AI
  • SynthV (Synthesizer V)

As much as VOCALOID is the first software where all the other synthesizers originated their base from, VOCALOID is not necessarily the most used software among them

What is the difference?

As mentioned previously, VOCALOID is created by Yamaha, however, the other synthesizers are made by other people and managed in different ways, for example, UTAU is a free and community driven synthesizer instead of being mainly managed by the developer of the software

Other than such a simple difference, a synthesizer like SynthV is said to be more realistic and AI-based as compared to the other synthesizers

For different reasons other than the ones stated, people used different synthesizers based on their preferences
